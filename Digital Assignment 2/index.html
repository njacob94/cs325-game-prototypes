Phaser Basic Project Template

 

Organ Surgeon

  • High Concept: Play a surgeon trying to safely remove organs from a patient without damaging the sensative areas
  • Theme:  Organ donar (losely)
  • Mandated Variety: Input: keyboard, randomness: a lot, Genre: top down, Play style: Competitor
  • Player Experience Goals: Trying to balance speed versus precision when removing organs.
  • Gameplay: The player begins in the low right with a knife, they must use the arrow keys to guide the knife to each organ and then guide the organ back to the operating table to get points.  A player gets 10 points if they get to an organ and a 100 points + remaining time on the clock when they drop it off.  Hitting the black blocks, representing sensative tissue, will reduce their score by 10 each time they hit it.  
  • Prototype Goal: I focused on randomizing the black area and organ placement to give it some replayability, I also focused on randomizing the movement of the player somewhat by giving the velocities some random range rather then a set number.
  • Features: The players hand is slighty randomized in terms of how fast it moves in each direction making it harder for the player to control their movement.  The black areas are also randomized to give it some replayability
  • Setting/Premise: Highly abstracted representation of a surgeon trying to remove organs from a patients body
  • Story: The player plays a surgeon in the ER, patients are brought to them and they have to try and remove organs as fast as possible without harming them.
  • Target Audience: Competing for scores with friends
  • Play Time: Up to a minute per round
  • Strategies: Trying to stick to the edges of the game board seems to help
  • Prototype Assets: Some assets are self made, the organs/knife/cutting sound were all taken from free sources
    • Knife: Minecraft knifes
    • Organs: Shutterstock
    • Hand: Pixel hand Icons
    • Cutting sound: Soundbible
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: This game had no particular prototype and was built from scratch.  Phaser's library was the only part used to learn how to use various commands
