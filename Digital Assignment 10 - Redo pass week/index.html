The Great Bookstore Heist

  • High Concept: Two players play a more complex prisoner's dilemma and have to weight working together to avoid being caught with stealing the most books
  • Mandated Variety: Clickable Buttons, deterministic, competitive, Adventure
  • Player Experience Goals: Players should be in a pyschological back and forth trying to decide when it is best to work together and when its best to stab each other in the back for more books
  • Gameplay: Each player can select 2 actions per turn from an array of actions.  These have various effects from stealing more books to fleeing to the getaway car.  At the end of a round the police try and stop them (odds are based on actions choosen earlier) and may or may not succeed 
  • Prototype Goal: Make sure core gameplay loop works and work out any logic bugs (of which this game wound up with a lot of) 
  • Features: Simple interface and gameplay, but compelling core gameplay loop that forms the basis for a good game.
  • Setting/Premise: Two bookworms trying to heist books from a book store
  • Target Audience: Simple Text Game meant for two players
  • Play Time: around 5 minutes, though bad luck early can end the game quickly
  • Strategies: Don't stab in the back to quickly or run the risk of not being safe enough to escape
  • Prototype Assets:  Only assets were the buttons, which were done by me
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: This is a digital prototype, based on the analog game I made from 2 weeks ago.  Still pretty buggy, but starting to come together.
