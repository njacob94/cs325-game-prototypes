Dungeon Text Adventure

  • High Concept: The player attempts to explore a dungeon.  Each turn they can press the advance button to move forward one room and encounter something new.  The goal is to accumlate as much treasure as possible
  • Theme: Iteration week
  • Mandated Variety: Clickable Buttons, Random, Explorre, Adventure
  • Player Experience Goals: Players should be taking tactical risks.  The main mechanic is deciding when to use mana to boost health or strength.  And when to decide to try and leave the dungeon early, or keep pushing forward.
  • Gameplay: Player has an array of buttons, they can press the advance a room in the dungeon.  When they move into a room they are presented with one of several different random encounters, ranging from getting treasure to battling monsters and doding traps. The player can spend treasure at a merchant to buy items or spend mana during the game to boost their stats or heal.  The main mechanic is balancing mana, spending treasure on gear and deciding when a run is going badly and they need to back out.  
  • Prototype Goal: Make sure core gameplay works compared to original card game.  Make sure the core gameplay loop is compelling.  Players should feel some anxiety about pressing the next room button when low health, and success as they get stronger and stonger and are more likely to be able to pass rooms.  
  • Features: Simple interface and gameplay, but compelling core gameplay loop that forms the basis for a good game.
  • Setting/Premise: Generic adventure game, little detail added, though setting is implied to be generic fantasy
  • Story:  No story at the moment, only dramatic elements relate to worry if the player's health is low.
  • Target Audience: Simple Text dungeon crawler 
  • Play Time: around 5 minutes, though bad luck early can end the game quickly
  • Strategies: Generally only boost health with mana when it's an emergency, boosting strength tends to be more important for survival.  From the merchant consider buying the sword first then the armor and potion.  The sword is a big advantage
  • Prototype Assets:  The audio used for when you beat a monster or boss was gotten from sound bible, All other (limited) assets were hand drawn by me.
